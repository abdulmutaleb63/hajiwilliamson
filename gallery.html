The Life of Haji Williamson

Few areas in Britain have produced as many notable families in world history. The family name Williamson is one of those. It is believed to be descended originally from the Strathclyde Britons — an ancient founding race of the north, a mixture of Gaelic and Celtic peoples whose original territories ranged from Lancashire in the south to the banks of the River Clyde in Scotland.

One of the most remarkable of this lineage was William Richard Williamson — known to the Arab world as Haji Abdullah Fadhil — an adventurer, trader, and bridge between East and West. His story traces an extraordinary journey from the ports of Bristol to the deserts of Arabia.
